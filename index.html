BRAHMANIC EXTREMISM
The Ultimate Truth Beyond Duality and Non-Duality

Supreme Non-Dual Reality

"Brahman is the only truth, the world is illusory, and the individual soul is non-different from Brahman."

EXTREMIST NON-DUAL PERSPECTIVE

All concepts - including 'truth', 'illusion', 'Brahman', and even 'non-duality' itself - are mere mental constructs that must be annihilated in the furnace of direct realization. The seeker must understand that even the statement "Brahman is the only truth" is a lie if taken as a final truth. The moment you conceptualize Brahman, you've already fallen into illusion. True liberation occurs when even the notion of 'liberation' is seen as bondage.

The world isn't just 'illusory' - this very act of labeling it as illusion is itself part of the illusion. The individual soul doesn't 'merge' with Brahman because there was never any separation to begin with. Even the concept of 'non-difference' is a trap that must be transcended.

The Illusion of Liberation

The extremist non-dual practitioner sees through all frameworks. They recognize that spiritual practices, scriptures, and even the desire for enlightenment are all part of the cosmic play (lila) of consciousness. The ultimate realization is that there is no 'realization' - for there is no one to realize anything.

When you say "I am Brahman", you create a duality between 'I' and 'Brahman'. When you say "There is no I", you still maintain a duality between 'nothingness' and 'somethingness'. True extremism lies in seeing that even these dichotomies are illusions.

Transcending Non-Duality

Most non-dualists secretly cling to the idea of 'non-duality' as a superior concept. The extremist sees this as the ultimate trap. The moment you label something as 'non-dual', you've created a dualistic framework where 'dual' and 'non-dual' exist as opposites.

True realization is beyond all positions - even the position of being 'positionless'. It is the complete collapse of all cognitive structures, where even the collapse itself is seen as part of the play. There is no 'truth' to grasp, no 'error' to reject.

ॐ

Final Absolution

In this extremist vision, the seeker must burn all maps, destroy all signposts, and dissolve all concepts. The statement about Brahman is not a teaching but a weapon to destroy all teachings. The world is not an illusion to be rejected, nor Brahman a reality to be grasped. All that remains is the ungraspable, which cannot even be called 'ungraspable'.

The extremist lives in total freedom, where even the freedom itself is recognized as bondage. They walk through the illusory world laughing at the cosmic joke - that there was never anyone here to understand any of this.

This extreme non-dual perspective represents one end of the philosophical spectrum in interpreting Advaita Vedanta. It should not be taken as a practical guide for spiritual practice, but rather as an intellectual exploration of absolute non-dualism.
